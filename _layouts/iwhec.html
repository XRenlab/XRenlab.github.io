Southern University of Science and Technology 		11.13下午 TBD 线下 Onsite The last decade has witnessed the blooming growth of healthcare chatbots in behavior interventions, elderly care, healthcare education, online diagnosis and so on. Considering that users tend to be more sensitive and vulnerable in healthcare services, it is necessary to investigate how to design healthcare chatbots by considering their social characteristics and communication modalities. This workshop aims to first discuss the theories and research in terms of how social characteristics and communication modalities would influence human-chatbot interaction in various healthcare service scenarios. It then introduces some commonly used participatory design methods and evaluation tools. Specifically, participants were invited to develop a set of social characteristics and modality cards, and employ the card tools to evoke user attitudes and perceptions towards human-chatbot interaction. A series of design guidelines is expected to be proposed to inform future healthcare chatbot design.
